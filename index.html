The Standard Visitor Visa is a UK short stay visa that allows you to stay in the UK for up to 6 months. Please note that the embassy has the right to issue visas for a shorter period of time.

You can also choose to apply for a long-term standard visitor visa if you visit the UK regularly. This visa lasts 2,5 or 10 years. You can stay for a maximum of 6 months on each visit.

You can apply for a UK Student Visa to study in the UK if you’re 18 or over and you:

  1. Have been offered a place on a course by a licenced student sponsor
  2. Have enough money to support yourself and pay for your course
  3. Can speak, read, write and understand English

How long you can stay depends on the length of your course and what study you’ve already completed in the UK

A Graduate Route Visa gives you permission to stay in the UK for at least 2 years after successfully completing a course in the UK.

You must be in the UK when you apply.

You can apply for a Start-Up Visa if you want to set up an innovative business in the UK.

Before you apply you need to have your business or business idea assessed by an endorsing body. They will provide you with an endorsement letter if your business is viable.

You must also:

  1. Be at least 18 years old
  2. Meet the English Language requirement
  3. Have had at least £1,270 in your bank account for 28 consecutive days before you apply

The UK High Potential Individual (HPI) route is a short-term work visa.

You can apply for the HPI route if you have graduated from an eligible international university in the 5 years immediately before your application.


In order to apply for this route, your application must have been verified by ECCTIS. If successful, you will be given a 2-year work visa (3-year for those with a PHD).
